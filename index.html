Vision Statement

Part 1: My Current Strengths & Areas for Growth

My current strengths at the beginning of the semester are my routine and time management. These are the skills that I brought with me to Northeastern University. I have a similar schedule for weekdays where I would wake up at 8 AM, get ready, eat breakfast, and go to class from 9 AM to 12 PM. After that, I will have lunch and I would have one more class from 1:35 PM until 2:40 PM. The library is usually where I go to study and work on assignments when I don’t have anything planned. I also love working out and I will go to the gym for around an hour right before I get dinner. At night, I sometimes will get more work done or play games to destress, and I will go to bed before midnight.

I currently consider myself a “student” because I’m still learning more about myself, my network, career options, and even my surroundings. I am taking classes to increase my knowledge of different soft and technical skills. One of my values is experience because I am more effective at learning by doing it and experimenting with it, in addition to visualization learning. That experience of me interacting around will allow me to learn from it. On the other hand, I feel networking and meeting new people is also important to me to increase my experience. I am interested in learning more about other people’s culture and their backgrounds. By interacting with other people, we are learning from each other as well, therefore they are shaping the way I am. Thus, I value new experiences and networking.

A change that I began to notice during the semester was that I tend to procrastinate less on homework and other activities. I used to have a very bad habit of getting distracted while doing work, therefore I would end up spending double the amount of time or even more on an assignment. Before coming to Northeastern, I had the mindset that college is going to be stressful, and that the workload is going to be stressful. Knowing that I procrastinate, I got into the habit of starting my assignment early, so even if I do procrastinate, I will still be able to finish on time and avoid stress at the last minute. Although the workload isn’t as bad as I thought, I believe that habit just pushes me to focus on the work and get it done before I do any fun activities.

One area that I need improvement in is my public speaking skills. I’ve always been shy and don’t like to stick out in the crowd. I grew up with social anxiety and every time I tried speaking publicly, I would stutter and forget what to say. Because of that, my communication skills are not the best and it is one thing that I hope to improve throughout the semester. One step that I can take is to take a class on public speaking. I believe that will help with my social anxiety and learn how to speak confidently. In addition, I attend mock interview sessions because not only will that improve my communication skills, but it can also prepare me for technical and job interviews.

Another area is my technical skills in computer science. I want to be ready for my first co-op session and be confident in my skills as a programmer. I also need to be ready for the interview since they will usually test on your technical knowledge such as data structures, algorithms, and different concepts. To prepare me for these, I would like to learn a new coding language to expose me to a different and new experience and create a project with the language to apply my knowledge into practice.

Part 2: My Long-Term Professional Goal

Upon graduation, I want to get into the job market. I expect that I will be working as a software developer/engineer since I enjoy coding, hence it’s one of the reasons that I am currently pursuing a Computer Science degree. Some of the dream companies that I would love to work for is Intel, AMD, and Nvidia, or one of the big tech companies (Apple, Facebook, Google, Microsoft, etc.). However, I will be satisfied in working for any company that has a good work balance life because I value more of the experience, I get out of the job rather than the prestige.

As a software engineer, the responsibilities can vary a lot depending on the focus of the field. For example, a mobile software engineer may focus on developing mobile applications on iOS or Android platforms, or a full-stack software engineer will focus on the entire development process of a piece of software. Currently, I don’t know which field I will be interested in working in, however through the class that I will take at Northeastern and the Co-op opportunities, I will eventually discover the type of field that I will enjoy working in.

My long-term professional goal is to become an entrepreneur and sell my idea. I want to be able to design a piece of software that will be able to improve the quality of life and everyday experience for people. This idea was inspired by how our lives were dramatically improved in terms of convenience and efficiency by all the apps around us. An example is Uber, which improved the convenience for getting around as well as provides a source of income. Since this is my long-term professional goal, I don’t expect to complete this soon, and getting a full-time job after graduation will be the first step.

Part 3: My Long-Term Personal Goals

After my graduation, I hope that I have completed at least two co-op opportunities and at least one study abroad session. With these in mind, there are a couple of things I would like to accomplish within the first couple of years post-graduation.

The first thing that I would like to accomplish is to travel abroad by myself. Growing up, I watched a lot of Travel Youtubers and read many travel blogs. I was fascinated by the different cultures and cool things that countries around the world have to offer. Therefore, after graduation, I want to experience what these Travel YouTubers experience and fulfill my childhood aspirations. The first location that I want to travel to by myself is China, specifically the lesser well-known cities. Whenever someone hears about traveling to China, they will always mention the popular big cities such as Beijing and Shanghai, as well as some of the popular attractions like the Great Wall of China and the Terracotta Army. However, when I have the opportunity to travel to China alone after graduation, I want to visit the lesser well-known city and experience more of China’s nature, in addition to the popular cities and attractions.

Another thing that I want to accomplish is to dedicate half a year to self-improvement. Throughout the years, I have been constantly having to deal with balancing schoolwork and personal life. However, somehow, I put more time into schoolwork, and I don’t think I have invested enough time in my personal life. There are so many things that I would like to improve such as fitness, diets, and even skin care routines. So, I would like to just dedicate 6 months to improve physical, mental, and emotional health.

Part 4: Competencies and Skills

My professional goal is to become an entrepreneur and create software to improve the daily lives of other people. Whether it is to improve the efficiency of daily life or the convenience of doing some tasks, or even some small quality of life improvement, I want to deliver that kind of improvement to people. To achieve this goal, there are many things’ competencies and skills that I will need to develop to achieve this goal.

The first thing that I need to establish is an understanding of what is daily life for many of us. From there I can identify areas of improvement and create a solution in the form of software. For example, the founder of Uber identifies a problem where it was too expensive to hire a private driver and wanted to create a solution that will make direct transportation easier and less expensive So, the founder created Uber, where you can book a ride to get you to your destination as well as share the price between another person who has a similar destination. Therefore, if I want to improve our daily life, I need to find out areas of improvement in our day.

Once I figure what I can improve, I need to think about how I can improve. This will surely require a lot of thinking and trial and error. After coming up with a solution, I will need some feedback and assess whether that solution will work in reality. I can get feedback from asking people who may benefit from this solution directly and indirectly. After that is sorted out, I can start working on developing the solution into a product. In this step, I will need enough coding knowledge and experience to make software out of the idea. So, in conclusion, I will need to find an issue, come up with a solution, asses the solution effectiveness, and then develop the solution.

My personal goal is to travel abroad by myself, more specifically traveling to China and visiting local areas/attractions in addition to big cities and popular attractions. One competency and skill that I will need is the understanding of Chinese. Since I will be traveling alone, it is best to understand the local language. I currently do know Chinese, but I am not very good at reading and writing Chinese. I can work to improve my Chinese right now by taking some Chinese language classes at Northeastern as well join clubs such as The Chinese Language Table. Other skills that I will need are saving and budgeting. Travel international is really expensive and it is important to spend my money wisely to have enough money left to cover necessary spending.

My other personal goal is self-improvement, and although I don’t need to develop competencies and skills for this goal, I still need to develop a good plan for me to accomplish the goal. Things like developing a fitness plan and coming up with ways that I can use to improve my physical, mental, and emotional health are necessary.

Part 5: Reflection

Many of these plans are focused on improvement to both myself and the people around me. My professional goal is to develop a piece of software that will improve the daily life of myself and the people around me. My personal goals consist of traveling abroad to improve my global competencies and self-improvement of mental and physical health. All of these goals reflect on my personality and values. I believe that I am a very considerate person and have a willingness to help others out. Therefore, through my professional goals, I will be able to help others out and possibly help myself out.

In the end, I want to contribute to society in a way that is possible using my skills and passion.
